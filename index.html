The Dark Web

This visualization explores anonymity on the highly connected Dark Web. Although sites on the darknet offer to protect the privacy of their visitors and owner, administrators can inadvertently leak details that put everyone at risk. Staying anonymous on the so-called dark web is hard. Even the most seasoned Darknet administrators like OxyMonster, can make one small mistake and be done for it.

This visualization use OnionScan to scan 8,176 .onion hidden services on the Tor anonymity network and gather various identifying metadata associated with them. We use this metadata in various ways within the hive table to explore relationships and patterns between hidden services using identity correlations such as IP addresses, SSH keys, PGP key fingerprints, related Clearnet sites.

These identities are positionally encoded on their own axes. Hidden services are positioned according to their weighted security score based on found keys, Apache mod_status leak, open directories, bitcoin addresses and server fingerprints. Clearnet sites are positional encoded based on degree and IP addresses are grouped by Class A.
